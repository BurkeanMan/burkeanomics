# Burkeanomics Simulator

Burkean Economics is a novel form of behavioral economics that models our very human world as a collection of particles.

There are four classes of Burkean particles: **Electrons** and three kinds of **Nucleons**. Like particle physics, they interact with each other in understandable ways — both within their class and across classes.

Burkeanomics models the economic world around you, with **you at the center**:

| Class | Who they are |
|---|---|
| **Electrons** | Families and singles |
| **GovNukes** | Governmental agencies |
| **Providers** | Schools, hospitals, businesses |
| **SinSayers** | Religions, movements, and moral arbiters |

The simulator compares three political scenarios — **cCon Left**, **Center**, and **dCon Right** — across all four classes, letting you tune parameters and see how each universe plays out in terms of BrainPower and Energy spend, per capita and en masse.

Electron Throttling is the primary way to model Left, Center & Right. Higher Electron Throttling comes from greater cCon, e.g., higher tax rates. Lower Electron Throttling comes from reduced cCon, i.e., dCon.

The pages in the References panel at the top of the page provide fundamental background on Burkeanomics in general and Burkeanomic modeling in particular.
